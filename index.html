The Filmmaker

Born
1994
Myanmar

In the face of oppression, stories become our most powerful weapon. Every frame I capture is a testament to the resilience of my people— those who refuse to be silenced.

2015

Detained and imprisoned during education reform protests as a student activist

2021

Joined protests in Yangon after the military coup, later fled to Lay Kay Kaw in Karen State

2021

Escaped to Thailand when the military invaded Lay Kay Kaw

Now

Founder of Premium Records • Documentary Filmmaker in Exile

As a documentary filmmaker and music video director from Myanmar, I document the untold stories of resistance, hope, and human dignity. Working from exile, I continue to shine light on the struggles of ordinary people caught in extraordinary circumstances— from the aftermath of natural disasters to the ongoing fight for freedom and justice.

COVID-19 and the Working Class: Post-Nargis Yangon

A five-part documentary series focusing on the lives of five families residing in the slum areas of Yangon, detailing the intertwined impact of COVID-19 and the aftermath of Cyclone Nargis.

Yangon COVID-19 Cyclone Nargis Working Class
2023
Documentary Film Feature

The Shattered Dream

Ko Saw Tun, Ma Thin Zar and their family reached Lay Kay Kaw in 2019 for their children's education. They built their house and lived their dreams. When the military invaded, they had to flee. A story of hope, displacement, and resilience.
